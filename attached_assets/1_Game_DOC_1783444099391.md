## SECTION 00: PHILOSOPHY OF PLAY  
  
### 0.1 — The Emotional Thesis  
  
**«95-Й» is a game about the quiet desperation of being a neighbor.** It is not a game about stealing fuel. It is a game about *knowing* someone is stealing fuel, *suspecting* everyone, and *realizing* that the polite fiction of courtyard life — the nods, the "добрый вечер", the borrowed sugar — is a thin film stretched over a Hobbesian war of each against all, fought with siphon hoses and voted on at сходки.  
  
The player should leave a session feeling *delighted paranoia*: the same feeling you get walking back to your car in a Russian courtyard at 2 AM and noticing the fuel cap is slightly ajar. Did someone touch it? Did you forget to close it? Are you being paranoid, or not paranoid enough?  
  
We are not making Among Us with Russian paint. We are making the first game that captures the specific texture of post-2024 Russian courtyard life — where inflation has made petrol a currency, where neighbors are suspects, and where the only honest transaction left is the one you do in USDT through CryptoBot at 3 AM.  
  
### 0.2 — Creative Pillars  
  
Every feature, asset, line of dialogue, and UI element must satisfy at least two of these pillars. If it satisfies only one, it is cut. If it satisfies none, it is forbidden.  
  
**Pillar I — Bright World, Dark Hearts.**  
The visual world is a children's-book courtyard: saturated sky, polished cars, blooming flowerpots. The *behavior* of the players is feral. We never darken the world to signal danger. The dissonance between the pretty scenery and the betrayal happening inside it *is* the comedy. This is the *RV There Yet?* principle: vacation visuals, vacation-ending chaos.  
  
**Pillar II — Every Mechanic Tells a Russian Joke.**  
No mechanic is abstract. "Tasks" are not wires to connect — they are shawarma to buy, grandmas to escort, intercoms to fix. "Sabotage" is not a bomb — it is бабушка-цербер blocking the entrance. Every system is legible to a Russian player as *something they have actually done or seen*. Satire is the substrate, not the decoration.  
  
**Pillar III — Five-Minute Sessions, Thirty-Year Memories.**  
A complete match is 5–10 minutes. But the stories players tell afterward — "and then Дядя Серёжа voted out Аня because she was 'walking too calmly'" — must be the kind of stories you tell at a kitchen table for years. We design for *anecdote generation*, not just engagement. Every match must contain at least one moment absurd enough to screenshot.  
  
**Pillar IV — The Сходка Is the Game.**  
The meeting phase is not a pause between action. It is the climax. 60% of development attention goes into the meeting UI, the chat wheel, the voting drama, and the ejection theatrics. The courtyard phase is the *setup*; the сходка is the *punchline*. Players must hunger for the meeting, not tolerate it.  
  
**Pillar V — Fuel Is the Universal Metaphor.**  
Fuel is the score. Fuel is the stakes. Fuel is what you steal, what you protect, what you panic about. By the end of a session, the player must feel in their bones that fuel is the only thing that matters — and then the CTA whispers: *"In real life, you can lock the price. @fuel_fuel_fuel_bot."* The product integration is not an ad; it is the philosophical punchline of the entire game.  
  
**Pillar VI — Mobile-First Means Thumb-First.**  
Every interaction is designed for one thumb on a phone in a metro. No pinch-zoom. No precise aiming. No keyboard. If a mechanic requires two thumbs, it must be optional. The virtual joystick is the spine of the experience. Everything else is a tap.  
  
**Pillar VII — Satire Without Cruelty.**  
We mock the situation, never the people. The fuel crisis is the villain, not the Russians living through it. Дядя Серёжа is funny because he brags about his tickets, not because he is stupid. Крипто-Вова is funny because he believes, not because he is broke. We punch up at Gazprom and inflation; we never punch down at the courtyard.  
  
### 0.3 — Anti-Pillars (What We Will Never Do)  
  
- **No dark atmospheric horror.** No vignettes, no film grain, no desaturated palettes. The world is bright. Betrayal happens in sunlight.  
- **No grinding.** No "play 50 matches to unlock a hat." Cosmetics are earned through play *moments*, not play *hours*.  
- **No pay-to-win.** Cosmetics only. A player who spends $0 and a player who spends $100 have identical gameplay.  
- **No generic Among Us clone language.** No "Crewmate," no "Impostor," no "Emergency Meeting." Every term is Russian-courtyard-specific. We say *Хозяин*, *Сливщик*, *Сходка*.  
- **No ads that break immersion.** The fuel ticket promotion is *inside* the fiction. There is no banner. There is no pre-roll. There is only the world, and the world happens to mention that you can lock the price.  
- **No procedural dialogue.** Every line is written by a human (or by me, now, in this document). No "Хозяин_1 says: I saw something suspicious." Every line has cadence, slang, and intent.  
  
### 0.4 — Player Personas  
  
We design for three players, in this order of priority:  
  
**Persona A — "Денис-Таксист" (Primary)**  
28 years old, drives Yandex Taxi in Kazan, plays Telegram games in the car between rides. Has 5–10 minute gaps. Wants quick matches, loud moments, shareable screenshots. Will share the game in his driver WhatsApp group if it makes him laugh. He is our viral vector. He does not read tutorials. He learns by dying once.  
  
**Persona B — "Аня-Студентка" (Secondary)**  
21 years old, university in St. Petersburg, plays with her TG group chat of 8 friends in the evening. Wants multiplayer, voice chat, drama. She is our retention engine. She will play 20 matches a week if her friends do. She cares about cosmetics and titles.  
  
**Persona C — "Дядя Серёжа" (Tertiary)**  
55 years old, retired, actually buys fuel tickets in real life. Plays single-player on a tablet. Wants slower pace, clear UI, large text. He is our conversion target. He will click the CTA. He will buy tickets. He will tell his wife.  
  
Every UI decision must work for Persona A (fast, thumb-only), Persona B (social, expressive), and Persona C (legible, slow).

## SECTION 01: WORLD BIBLE  
  
### 1.1 — The Setting  
  
**ЖК "Цветочные Поляны"** (Residential Complex "Flower Fields") is a real place in our fiction. It exists somewhere on the outskirts of a Russian regional city — Kazan, Yekaterinburg, Voronezh, it doesn't matter which. What matters is that it is *every* new Russian ЖК: the same 25-story monolithic panels, the same fresh asphalt with the same cracks appearing in year two, the same playground that cost 8 million rubles and is used by exactly two children, the same parking lot where the same eight cars park in the same eight spots every night.  
  
The time is *now*. Summer 2026. The price of АИ-95 is 85₽/liter and rising 2₽ every week. The price of diesel is higher. The government says inflation is 8.2%. Everyone in the courtyard knows it is closer to 24%. The Telegram channel of the ЖК has 312 members. The last pinned message is from the старшая by домоуправление: *"Уважаемые жильцы! Прошу не сливать бензин с чужих автомобилей. Это уголовное преступление. — Администрация."* It has 47 laughing-reaction emojis.  
  
The courtyard is always in golden hour. The sun is warm. The sky is #87CEEB. The flowerpots on the balconies are blooming. It is a beautiful place to live, if you don't mind that someone is stealing your fuel.

### 1.4 — The Satire Layer (Real-World Integration)  
  
The game pulls live data from public sources to make the satire *current*:  
  
1. **Fuel Price Ticker (top of HUD):** Pulls from a static JSON (updated weekly by us manually, with fallback) showing current АИ-95, ДТ, and USDT-to-ruble rates. If API fails, uses last cached values. Never breaks gameplay.  
2. **News Ticker (bottom of HUD):** Rotates through 50 hand-written satirical headlines (see §1.5). Updated each season.  
3. **"Сегодня в ЖК" (Today in the complex):** A rotating flavor text on the main menu: "В подъезде №3 снова сломался лифт. Администрация: 'Заявка в работе.'" — purely atmospheric, updated weekly.  
  
This layer grounds the game in *the present moment*. When a player sees "АИ-95: 87₽↑" in the ticker while their car is being drained, the satire lands.

## SECTION 02: CORE GAMEPLAY LOOP  
  
### 2.1 — Phase Architecture  
  
A match of 95-Й progresses through five phases. Each phase has a defined duration, UI state, and server authority.  
  
```  
LOBBY (variable, 30-120s)  
  ↓ (host clicks START, or 8 players join)  
ROLE ASSIGNMENT (instant, server-side)  
  ↓  
BRIEFING (5s, cinematic)  
  ↓  
PLAY PHASE (5-10 min, or until win condition)  
  ↓ (meeting called)  
СХОДКА (60-90s, discussion + vote)  
  ↓  
RESOLUTION (5s, ejection cinematic)  
  ↓ (return to PLAY, or GAME OVER)  
```  
  
**Phase Specifications:**  
  
**LOBBY:**  
- Players join via Telegram deep link (`t.me/bakstab_bot?startapp=ROOM_XXXXXX`).  
- Lobby UI: list of joined players (avatar + name), chat box, "READY" button.  
- Host sees "START" button (enabled at 4+ players).  
- Lobby auto-starts at 8 players after 10 seconds.  
- Lobby expires after 5 minutes of inactivity.  
  
**ROLE ASSIGNMENT (server-side, instant):**  
- Server selects Сливщик count based on player count:  
  - 4 players: 1 Сливщик  
  - 5-6 players: 1 Сливщик  
  - 7-8 players: 2 Сливщики  
- Сливщики are informed of each other. Хозяева are not informed of roles.  
- Neutrals (8-player lobbies only): 1 random neutral role (see §3.1.3).  
- Server sends `role_assigned` packet to each client privately.  
  
**BRIEFING:**  
- 5-second cinematic: top-down view of the courtyard, camera pans from above.  
- Text overlay: "ЖК «Цветочные Поляны». Лето 2026. АИ-95: 85₽. Кто-то сифонит ваши баки."  
- For Сливщики: additional text: "ВЫ — СЛИВЩИК. Слейте все баки. Не попадитесь."  
- For Хозяева: "ВЫ — ХОЗЯИН. Заполните индикатор двора. Вычислите сифонеров."  
- Skip button appears after 2 seconds (for Persona A who hates waiting).  
  
**PLAY PHASE:**  
- The core gameplay. See §2.2-2.6 for full spec.  
- Duration: 5 minutes default, +30 seconds per task completed (extends slightly to allow comebacks).  
- Win conditions checked every server tick (20Hz).  
  
**СХОДКА (Meeting):**  
- Triggered by: body discovery, drained car discovery, alarm button at Entrance Arch.  
- See §2.7 for full spec.  
- Duration: 60 seconds discussion + 30 seconds voting = 90 seconds max.  
  
**RESOLUTION:**  
- 5-second cinematic of the ejection (see §2.7.4).  
- If game continues, return to PLAY with a 3-second "Сходка окончена" overlay.  
  
### 2.2 — Movement & Camera  
  
**Coordinate System:**  
- World coordinates in meters, origin (0,0) at courtyard center.  
- Y-axis points "north" (away from entrance).  
- Player position is a 2D vector `(x, y)` with `angle` (facing direction, radians).  
  
**Movement Model:**  
- Base speed: 3.5 m/s (walking).  
- Sprint: 5.5 m/s, drains a "stamina" bar (5 seconds sprint, 8 seconds regen). Stamina is invisible to other players — only you see your own.  
- Crouch: 1.8 m/s, reduces vision cone of others seeing you by 30% (stealth).  
- Diagonal movement: normalized to base speed (no Quake III strafe-jumping).  
  
**Camera:**  
- Top-down, follows player.  
- Visible radius: 12 meters around player.  
- Vision cone: 140° in front of player. Outside the cone, objects are rendered at 40% opacity (peripheral vision).  
- Behind walls: completely invisible (no x-ray).  
- Сливщики have 160° cone (slightly better — the darkness in their hearts).  
- Camera smoothing: lerp at 0.15 (15% per frame at 60fps) — smooth but responsive.  
  
**Input (Mobile-First):**  
- Left thumb: virtual joystick (anywhere on left half of screen). Joystick appears where thumb lands. Drag direction = movement direction. Drag magnitude = speed (analog, 0 to 1).  
- Right thumb: drag to rotate facing direction. Tap = interact. Long-press (180ms) = siphon/hold-action.  
- Right thumb swipe up = quick-emote wheel (4 emotes: 👋, 🤔, 🚨, 😂).  
- Double-tap right = sprint toggle.  
- No virtual buttons on screen during PLAY phase (except interaction prompt, which appears contextually).  
  
**Input (Desktop Fallback):**  
- WASD: movement.  
- Mouse: aim/look.  
- E: interact.  
- F: hold to siphon.  
- Q: emote wheel.  
- Shift: sprint.  
- Ctrl: crouch.  
- Space: alarm (at Entrance Arch only).  
  
### 2.3 — Vision System  
  
The vision system is the heart of social deduction. If you can't see, you can't witness. If you can't witness, you can't accuse.  
  
**Vision Algorithm (per player, per frame):**  
  
1. Cast 36 rays from player position, 5° apart (covers 180° in front).  
2. Each ray extends up to 12 meters or until it hits a vision-blocker (car, dumpster, wall, building).  
3. Build a polygon from the ray endpoints. This polygon is the player's visible area.  
4. Other players are visible *only if* their center point is inside this polygon AND not behind a vision-blocker from the player's perspective.  
5. Dead bodies and drained cars are visible under the same rules.  
6. The polygon is also used to cull rendering — only visible objects are drawn. This is a performance optimization as well as a gameplay mechanic.  
  
**Edge Cases:**  
- If two players are on opposite sides of a car, neither sees the other.  
- If a player is crouching behind a dumpster, they are invisible to everyone outside the dumpster zone.  
- Сливщики siphoning a car are visible to anyone whose vision polygon includes the car.  
- A player inside the dumpster zone can see out, but cannot be seen in. This is the stealth zone.  
  
**Performance:**  
- Ray casting: 36 rays × 8 players = 288 rays per frame. At 60fps, that's 17,280 rays/sec. Trivial for modern phones. If profiling shows bottleneck, reduce to 24 rays (7.5° apart).

### 2.5 — Task System (Хозяева Objective)  
  
Tasks are the Хозяева's path to victory. Each task is a 5-15 second mini-game. Completing all tasks across all Хозяева fills the **Индикатор Двора (Courtyard Unity Meter)** from 0% to 100%. At 100%, Хозяева win.  
  
**Task Design Principles:**  
- Every task is a Russian courtyard joke.  
- Every task is completable with one thumb.  
- Every task has a satisfying "ka-ching" sound on completion.  
- Every task shows a progress bar to nearby players (so they can see you're "working").  
- Сливщики see the same task UI but their "completion" is a fake animation that doesn't add to the meter.

### 2.6 — Siphoning System (Сливщик Objective)  
  
Siphoning is the Сливщик's primary action. It is the most mechanically complex system in the game because it must feel *visceral, risky, and satisfying*.  
  
**Siphoning Phases:**  
  
**Phase 1 — Approach (0s):**  
- Сливщик enters interaction radius of a target car.  
- Prompt: "[HOLD] Слить бензин".  
- No visual or audio cue to other players yet.  
  
**Phase 2 — Setup (0-3s):**  
- Сливщик holds the button.  
- A subtle animation: Сливщик crouches, pulls out a hose.  
- Audio: A faint "click" of the fuel cap opening. Audible only within 3 meters.  
- If another player's vision polygon includes the Сливщик at this moment, they see a small ⚠️ icon above the Сливщик's head (subtle, not a full alert).  
- If the Сливщик releases before 3 seconds, the action cancels with no evidence.  
  
**Phase 3 — Active Drain (3-10s):**  
- The siphon is now "live".  
- Audio: A loud, looped "GURGLE" sound. Audible within 8 meters. This is the giveaway.  
- Visual: A green liquid stream flows from the car's fuel tank to a canister on the ground next to the Сливщик.  
- The car's fuel level drops at a rate of `car_max_fuel / 7` per second (full drain in 7 seconds).  
- A progress bar appears above the car (visible to all players who can see the car).  
- The Сливщик cannot move during this phase. Releasing the button cancels the drain but leaves the canister on the ground as evidence.  
  
**Phase 4 — Complete (10s):**  
- The car's fuel reaches 0%.   
- The canister is now "full" (a bright green prop on the ground).  
- The Сливщик can pick up the canister (carrying it slows movement by 20%).  
- The Сливщик must dispose of the canister by dropping it in the dumpsters (stealth) or hiding it behind bushes.  
- If a Хозяин sees a Сливщик carrying a canister, they can call a сходка immediately.  
  
**Fuel Math:**  
- Each car has a `max_fuel` of 50 liters (display) or 100% (internal).  
- Drain rate: `14.3% per second` (7 seconds to full drain from 100%).  
- Partial drains are possible: if a Сливщик drains for 4 seconds, the car loses 57% and the canister has 57% fill.  
- A car with <10% fuel triggers a "low fuel" warning to its owner (red icon on their HUD).  
- A car with 0% fuel is "drained" — the owner's heart breaks (they "die" emotionally, become a ghost).  
  
**Siphon Cooldown:**  
- After completing or canceling a siphon, the Сливщик has a 15-second cooldown before they can siphon again.  
- This prevents rapid-fire draining and forces planning.  
  
**The "Ambush" (Kill) Mechanic:**  
- Сливщики can "Ambush" (kill) a Хозяин who is alone (no other living player within 8 meters).  
- Tap the target player when adjacent (within 1.5m).  
- Animation: 1.5-second struggle (Сливщик pounces, target falls).  
- Body remains on the ground as a "Тело" interactable.  
- Ambush cooldown: 25 seconds.  
- If a third player walks within vision range during the struggle, the Ambush is interrupted and the target survives (the Сливщик is now visibly "suspicious" for 5 seconds — red outline).  
  
### 2.7 — The Сходка (Meeting) System  
  
The сходка is the climax. 60% of design attention is here.  
  
**2.7.1 — Triggers:**  
- Body discovered (Тело interacted with).  
- Drained car discovered (<10% fuel car interacted with).  
- Alarm button pressed at Entrance Arch.  
- Cooldown between сходки: 30 seconds (in PLAY phase).  
  
**2.7.2 — Transition:**  
- On trigger: server pauses PLAY phase, teleports all living players to the Entrance Arch.  
- 2-second transition cinematic: camera zooms out, shows all players gathering.  
- Audio: A loud "ATTENTION" horn + a babushka voice: "А ну-ка, собрались!"  
  
**2.7.3 — Meeting UI:**  
- All living players stand in a circle around the Entrance Arch.  
- Center of screen: large chat box (text + quick-chat wheel).  
- Right side: list of living players with their avatars. Tap to vote.  
- Top: timer (60s discussion + 30s voting = 90s total).  
- Bottom: quick-chat wheel (12 phrases, see §2.7.5).  
  
**2.7.4 — Voting:**  
- After 60 seconds of discussion, voting opens (or skip discussion by majority tap).  
- Each player taps a target avatar to vote. Tap "SKIP" to abstain.  
- Vote is hidden until the 30-second voting window closes.  
- On close: votes are revealed. Plurality wins. Ties = no ejection.  
- Ejected player is removed from the game.  
- Ejection cinematic: 5-second animation of the ejected player being placed in a trash chute. Satirical text overlay (see §2.7.6).  
- Ejected player's role is revealed: "Денис был Сливщиком." or "Аня не была Сливщицей."

### 2.8 — Win Conditions  
  
**Хозяева win if:**  
- Unity Meter reaches 100%, OR  
- All Сливщики are ejected at сходки.  
  
**Сливщики win if:**  
- All cars reach 0% fuel, OR  
- Хозяев count is reduced to or below Сливщиков count (via ambushes + ejections), OR  
- A critical sabotage is not resolved within 30 seconds (see §2.9).  
  
**Neutrals win if:**  
- Their personal win condition is met (see §3.1.3).  
  
### 2.9 — Sabotage System  
  
Сливщики have a radial sabotage menu (accessed by long-pressing the right side of the screen). Each sabotage has a cooldown.  
  
**Sabotage 01 — "Бабушка-Цербер" (Grandma Cerberus)**  
- Cooldown: 60 seconds.  
- Effect: A babushka NPC spawns at the Entrance Arch. She blocks the alarm button. Players cannot call a сходка until she is "fed" a shawarma (any player must bring a shawarma from the Shawarma Stand and tap her).  
- Duration: Until resolved, or 45 seconds (then she leaves on her own).  
- Satire: "Бабушка из 5 подъезда опять всех допрашивает."  
  
**Sabotage 02 — "Прорвало трубу" (Pipe burst)**  
- Cooldown: 75 seconds.  
- Effect: Water floods the parking lot. Cars cannot be interacted with (siphoned or checked) until 2 Хозяева simultaneously complete a "valve turning" mini-game at two valves (one at (-20, 5), one at (20, 5)).  
- Duration: Until resolved, or 60 seconds (then Хозяева lose — critical sabotage).  
- Satire: "Кто-то наступил на трубу. Опять."  
  
**Sabotage 03 — "ЖК Чат Офлайн" (Chat offline)**  
- Cooldown: 50 seconds.  
- Effect: Disables the alarm button and body reporting for 20 seconds. Сливщики can drain freely.  
- Satire: "Ростелеком опять порвал кабель."

## SECTION 04: AI ARCHITECTURE (SINGLE PLAYER)  
  
Single-player mode pits 1 human player against 3-7 AI bots. The AI must feel like real players — suspicious, fallible, sometimes brilliant, sometimes dumb.  
  
### 4.1 — AI Behavior Tree  
  
Each AI bot runs a behavior tree with the following nodes (evaluated every 0.5 seconds):  
  
```  
ROOT  
├── [CHECK] Am I dead? → YES: Ghost behavior (wander, observe)  
│  
├── [CHECK] Is сходка active? → YES: Meeting behavior (see §4.4)  
│  
├── [CHECK] Am I a Сливщик?   
│   ├── YES: Сливщик behavior (see §4.2)  
│   └── NO: Хозяин behavior (see §4.3)  
│  
└── [FALLBACK] Wander behavior (default)  
```  
  
### 4.2 — Сливщик AI Behavior  
  
```  
СЛИВЩИК AI:  
├── [CHECK] Is a Хозяин within 8m and alone (no others within 12m)?  
│   └── YES (25% chance): AMBUSH target. Cooldown 25s.  
│  
├── [CHECK] Is a car available (not locked, not 0%, no witnesses within vision range)?  
│   └── YES (70% chance): SIPHON car. Choose car with highest fuel.  
│       └── After siphon: dispose of canister at dumpsters.  
│  
├── [CHECK] Is sabotage available (off cooldown)?  
│   └── YES (30% chance): TRIGGER sabotage (random choice, weighted by situation).  
│  
├── [CHECK] Am I being watched (Хозяин within vision cone, facing me)?  
│   └── YES: FAKE TASK (go to nearest task terminal, animate, do not complete).  
│  
├── [CHECK] Is сходка active?  
│   └── YES: Meeting behavior (see §4.4)  
│  
└── [FALLBACK] Wander to random task terminal, fake-task for 5-10 seconds.  
```  
  
**Сливщик AI Difficulty Tiers:**  
- **Лёгкий (Easy):** 70% chance to siphon when available. 20% chance to ambush. Never sabotages. Walks in straight lines.  
- **Средний (Medium):** 85% chance to siphon. 30% ambush. 50% sabotage. Avoids witnesses.  
- **Сложный (Hard):** 95% siphon. 50% ambush. 80% sabotage. Uses dumpsters as vents. Fakes tasks convincingly. Targets the player's car specifically if the player is winning.  
- **Кошмар (Nightmare):** 100% siphon. 70% ambush. 100% sabotage. Coordinates with second Сливщик (in 8-player). Stalks the player. Never fakes tasks in the same location twice.  
  
### 4.3 — Хозяин AI Behavior  
  
```  
ХОЗЯИН AI:  
├── [CHECK] Did I just witness a siphon/ambush?  
│   └── YES: Run to Alarm Button, call сходка. Report witness in chat.  
│  
├── [CHECK] Is a body within my vision?  
│   └── YES: Run to body, report it.  
│  
├── [CHECK] Is a car within 3m with <10% fuel?  
│   └── YES: Report drained car.  
│  
├── [CHECK] Is a suspicious player within 5m (recently near a drained car)?  
│   └── YES (40% chance): Follow them for 5 seconds. If they approach a car, call сходка.  
│  
├── [CHECK] Am I near an incomplete task?  
│   └── YES (80% chance): COMPLETE task.  
│  
├── [CHECK] Is Unity Meter >80%?  
│   └── YES: Prioritize task completion over investigation.  
│  
└── [FALLBACK] Wander to nearest incomplete task.  
```  
  
**Хозяин AI Suspicion System:**  
Each Хозяин AI maintains a suspicion vector for every other player:  
```  
suspicion[other_player] += 0.1 if other_player was seen near a drained car  
suspicion[other_player] += 0.2 if other_player was seen "siphoning" (caught in the act)  
suspicion[other_player] += 0.05 if other_player was seen running (sprint) for >3 seconds  
suspicion[other_player] += 0.05 if other_player skipped a task terminal without completing  
suspicion[other_player] -= 0.1 if other_player completed a task near me  
suspicion[other_player] -= 0.05 if other_player is Дядя Серёжа (ageism bias — satire)  
```  
  
At сходка, AI votes for the player with the highest suspicion (above threshold 0.3). If no one exceeds threshold, AI skips.  
  
### 4.4 — AI Meeting Behavior  
  
```  
AI MEETING:  
├── [CHECK] Am I the one reported?  
│   └── YES: Defend. Send 1-2 chat wheel phrases claiming alibi.  
│  
├── [CHECK] Am I a Сливщик?  
│   └── YES: Accuse the player with the highest suspicion (deflect). Send 2-3 phrases.  
│  
├── [CHECK] Did I witness something?  
│   └── YES: Report it. Send 1 phrase: "Я видел [X] у [location]."  
│  
├── [CHECK] Do I have high suspicion on someone?  
│   └── YES (60% chance): Accuse them. Send 1 phrase.  
│  
└── [FALLBACK] Send 1 random phrase. Vote based on suspicion vector.  
```  
  
**AI Chat Personality:**  
Each AI bot uses their character's voice lines (see §1.3) in chat. Денис says "Смена горит." Вова says "To the moon, bro." This makes the сходка feel alive, not robotic.  
  
### 4.5 — Pathfinding  
  
A* algorithm on a navmesh of the courtyard. Navmesh is precomputed (walkable polygons, with obstacles as holes). AI bots replan every 1 second or when their target moves.  
  
**Optimization:**  
- Bots within 12 meters of the player: full pathfinding, smooth movement.  
- Bots beyond 12 meters (not visible): simplified pathfinding (straight line + obstacle avoidance). Their position updates at 5Hz instead of 20Hz.

## SECTION 05: MULTIPLAYER ARCHITECTURE  
  
### 5.1 — Authoritative Server Model  
  
The server is authoritative. Clients send inputs, the server simulates the game, and broadcasts state. Clients never send "I drained a car" — they send "I held the siphon button on car X" and the server decides if the drain happened.  
  
**Why:** This prevents all client-side cheats (speed hacks, teleport, instant siphon). The client can lie about its position, but the server clamps it to valid movement. The client can lie about interactions, but the server validates timing, distance, and cooldowns.  
  
### 5.2 — Network Protocol  
  
WebSocket binary frames, MessagePack-encoded. 20Hz server tick rate (50ms per tick).  
  
**Client → Server Packets:**  
```typescript  
type ClientPacket =  
  | { t: 'move'; x: number; y: number; a: number; s: number }  // position, angle, speed  
  | { t: 'interact'; target: string; action: string }  
  | { t: 'siphon'; car: string; hold: boolean }  
  | { t: 'meeting'; reason: string; target?: string }  
  | { t: 'vote'; target: string | null }  
  | { t: 'chat'; msg: string }  
  | { t: 'emote'; e: number }  
  | { t: 'sabotage'; s: string };  
```  
  
**Server → Client Packets:**  
```typescript  
type ServerPacket =  
  | { t: 'state'; players: PlayerState[]; cars: CarState[]; meter: number; phase: string }  
  | { t: 'event'; e: string; data: any }  // siphon_start, kill, sabotage, etc.  
  | { t: 'meeting'; reason: string; reporter: string }  
  | { t: 'meeting_end'; ejected: string | null; wasDrainer: boolean }  
  | { t: 'game_over'; winner: string; stats: MatchStats }  
  | { t: 'role'; role: string }  // sent once at match start  
  | { t: 'error'; msg: string };  
```  
  
**State Broadcast:**  
Every 50ms, the server broadcasts the full game state to all clients. State includes:  
- All players: `id, x, y, angle, character, hat, alive, isSiphoning, isCarryingCanister`  
- All cars: `id, x, y, fuel, locked, drained`  
- Unity Meter: `0-100`  
- Phase: `play | meeting | resolution`  
- Sabotages: `active list with timers`  
  
State size: ~2KB per packet for 8 players. At 20Hz, that's 40KB/s per client. Trivial.  
  
### 5.3 — Lag Compensation  
  
**Client-Side Prediction:**  
- Client predicts its own movement locally (immediate response).  
- Server confirms or corrects. If correction > 0.5 meters, client snaps (rubber-banding). This is rare on good connections.  
  
**Interpolation:**  
- Other players are rendered at a 100ms delay, interpolated between the last 2 server states. This smooths jitter.  
  
**Rewind for Voting:**  
- When a сходка is called, the server pauses for 200ms to let all clients sync. This ensures all players see the same state when voting.  
  
### 5.4 — Reconnection Logic  
  
If a player disconnects (Telegram WebView crash, network loss):  
- **During Lobby:** Player is removed. Lobby continues.  
- **During Play (first 30 seconds):** Match is cancelled. Players return to lobby.  
- **During Play (after 30 seconds):** Player's character is taken over by AI (bot difficulty = Medium). Player can rejoin within 60 seconds via the same deep link. If they don't rejoin, they stay AI for the rest of the match.  
- **During Сходка:** Player's vote is recorded as SKIP. They can rejoin after the сходка.  
- **After Game Over:** Reconnect is moot; stats are recorded.  
  
### 5.5 — Matchmaking  
  
**Quick Play:**  
- Player taps "Быстрая игра" in the main menu.  
- Server maintains a queue. When 4-8 players are queued (within 200ms ping), a room is created.  
- Region: Single region (Frankfurt) for launch. Expand later.  
  
**Custom Room:**  
- Player taps "Создать комнату". Room code generated (6-char alphanumeric).  
- Player shares deep link: `t.me/bakstab_bot?startapp=ROOM_ABC123`.  
- Friends join via the link. Host taps START.  
  
### 5.6 — Anti-Cheat  
  
Server validates everything:  
- **Movement:** Speed clamped to 5.5 m/s (sprint). Teleport detection: if distance moved > 6m in one tick (impossible), flag and snap back.  
- **Interactions:** Distance check (must be within 1.5m of target). Cooldown check (siphon 15s, ambush 25s).  
- **Siphoning:** Server tracks fuel levels. Client cannot modify them.  
- **Voting:** One vote per player. Server-enforced.  
  
**Reporting:**  
- Players can report suspicious behavior via a "Пожаловаться" button in the post-match screen.  
- Reports are logged. Manual review for launch. Automated detection later.

### 6.6 — Frontend-Canvas Bridge Architecture  
  
React handles the UI (menus, HUD, сходка screen). Canvas handles the game rendering (60fps). They communicate via a Zustand store.  
  
```  
React Component Tree:  
<App>  
  <MainMenu />           # React, when phase = 'menu'  
  <Lobby />              # React, when phase = 'lobby'  
  <GameView>             # Always mounted during play  
    <Canvas />           # The game canvas (full screen)  
    <HUD />              # React overlay (fuel meter, minimap, prompts)  
    <ChatWheel />        # React overlay (during сходка)  
    <MeetingScreen />    # React overlay (during сходка)  
    <DeathScreen />      # React overlay (when dead)  
    <GameResults />      # React overlay (when game over)  
  </GameView>  
</App>  
```  
  
**The Canvas runs independently at 60fps.** It reads from a `gameStateRef` (mutable, not React state — React state would cause re-renders at 60fps and kill performance). React components read from Zustand stores that update at 10Hz (for HUD) or on events (for prompts, meetings).  
  
**The bridge:**  
1. WebSocket client receives `state` packet → writes to `gameStateRef.current`.  
2. Canvas render loop reads `gameStateRef.current` every frame → draws.  
3. WebSocket client receives `event` packet → updates Zustand store (for UI prompts).  
4. React user input (tap interact button) → calls `gameEngine.interact()` → sends WebSocket packet.  
  
This separation ensures the canvas never waits for React, and React never re-renders 60 times per second.

### 9.2 — Replay System ("Бакстаб Момент")  
  
**Mechanic:**  
- The client continuously records the last 10 seconds of gameplay in a circular buffer (canvas frames at 10 FPS, low quality JPEG).  
- When a "Бакстаб Момент" is detected (player catches a Сливщик in the act, or player is ejected), the buffer is saved as a GIF.  
- Player is prompted: "Сохранить Бакстаб Момент?" → Yes → downloads GIF.  
- GIFs are watermarked with "95-Й @bakstab_bot".  
  
**Detection:**  
- `catch_siphoner`: Player witnesses a siphon in progress (sees the green stream).  
- `caught_siphoning`: Player (as Сливщик) is witnessed.  
- `dramatic_eject`: Player is ejected at сходка.  
  
**Implementation:**  
```javascript  
class ReplayBuffer {  
  private frames: ImageData[] = [];  
  private maxFrames = 100; // 10 seconds at 10 FPS  
    
  capture(ctx: CanvasRenderingContext2D) {  
    const frame = ctx.getImageData(0, 0, ctx.canvas.width, ctx.canvas.height);  
    this.frames.push(frame);  
    if (this.frames.length > this.maxFrames) this.frames.shift();  
  }  
    
  saveAsGif(): Blob {  
    // Use gif.js library to encode  
    const gif = new GIF({ workers: 2, quality: 10, width: 400, height: 400 });  
    for (const frame of this.frames) {  
      // Downscale to 400x400, add to gif  
      gif.addFrame(downscale(frame, 400, 400), { delay: 100 });  
    }  
    return gif.finish();  
  }  
}  
```

### 12.4 — Risk Mitigation  
  
**Risk 01 — Server overload.**  
- Mitigation: Auto-scale on Hetzner (add VPS instances via Cloudflare load balancer). Redis handles room state, so adding servers is stateless.  
- Trigger: If CPU > 70% for 5 minutes, spin up new instance.  
  
**Risk 02 — Telegram API changes.**  
- Mitigation: Pin Telegram WebApp SDK version. Test on every Telegram app update. Maintain a fallback (open in external browser).  
  
**Risk 03 — Fuel ticket bot goes down.**  
- Mitigation: The game is fully playable without the fuel ticket integration. The "Immunity Ticket" power-up can be obtained in-game (random spawn). The CTA is non-blocking.  
  
**Risk 04 — Game is too hard to learn.**  
- Mitigation: First-time players get a 30-second interactive tutorial (walk to the shawarma stand, buy shawarma, return). Skippable but encouraged.  
  
**Risk 05 — Satire offends someone.**  
- Mitigation: Follow Pillar VII (Satire Without Cruelty). Mock the situation, not the people. Test with Russian playtesters before launch. If a line feels mean, cut it.

## SECTION 13: ACCESSIBILITY & LOCALIZATION  
  
### 13.1 — Accessibility  
  
**Visual:**  
- Colorblind mode: toggles fuel liquid color from green to blue (deuteranopia-friendly).  
- Text size: 3 levels (small, medium, large). Persona C (Дядя Серёжа) needs large.  
- High contrast mode: increases UI text contrast.  
- Minimap: always available (top-right). Players with poor vision can rely on it.  
  
**Motor:**  
- Tap zones are minimum 44×44px (Apple HIG).  
- No precision aiming required.  
- Sprint is a toggle, not a hold.  
- Auto-interact: if player is within interaction radius for 2 seconds, auto-trigger (optional, for one-handed play).  
  
**Audio:**  
- Subtitles for all voice lines (during сходка).  
- Visual indicator for siphon sound (on-screen ⚠️ when a siphon is audible within 8m, even if the player can't see it).  
- Volume controls: master, music, sfx. Separate sliders.  
  
**Cognitive:**  
- Tutorial mode (first-time player).  
- "Что делать?" (What to do?) button in HUD — shows current objective.  
- Simplified chat wheel (6 phrases instead of 12) for new players.  
  
### 13.2 — Localization  
  
**Primary:** Russian (all text, voice, satire).  
**Secondary:** English (UI text only — satire doesn't translate, but the game is playable).  
**Future:** Ukrainian, Kazakh (depending on traction).  
  
**Localization Strategy:**  
- Russian is the source language. All jokes are written in Russian first.  
- English translation is functional, not literary. The game is playable in English, but the *soul* is Russian.  
- Satirical headlines are NOT translated — they remain in Russian even in English mode (they're flavor text, not gameplay-critical). This preserves the cultural specificity.

## SECTION 14: ETHICAL DESIGN (ANTI-DARK-PATTERN MANIFESTO)  
  
We commit to the following. Violations are bugs.  
  
1. **No artificial friction.** We don't make the game worse to sell solutions. The fuel ticket power-up is a bonus, not a necessity.  
2. **No gambling.** No loot boxes with random odds. Cosmetics are purchased directly. The "daily spin" gives a guaranteed reward (just varies which hat).  
3. **No FOMO.** Seasonal cosmetics return in future seasons (different color variants). No "exclusive forever" items.  
4. **No notification spam.** The bot sends max 1 push per day (daily challenge reminder, opt-outable).  
5. **No data selling.** We collect only what's needed for gameplay. No third-party analytics (Plausible is self-hosted, privacy-friendly).  
6. **Honest difficulty.** AI doesn't cheat. It uses the same vision system as players. No "rubber-banding."  
7. **Transparent odds.** If we ever add loot boxes (we won't, but if we do), odds are displayed.  
8. **Easy exit.** Players can delete their account and all data via a `/delete` command in the bot. One click. No retention dark patterns.
